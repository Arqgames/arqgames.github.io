130 Years from Creation — Birth of Seth and the Chronological Anchor in the Bible

The first clear chronological anchor in the biblical timeline is the event of Adam begetting Seth at the age of 130 (Genesis 5). This age is based on the Masoretic Text and is the first explicitly stated chronological data within the Bible.

The Significance of Seth's Birth and Genealogies

This genealogy is reflected in works such as Josephus's "Antiquities of the Jews" and the Gospel of Luke, showing that Seth's lineage was understood as the legitimate bloodline. Genesis 7 also records Noah's age at the Flood, allowing calculation of the total years from Creation to the Flood. According to the Masoretic Text, this total is 1,656 years.

Differences in Transmission

However, these numbers differ by textual tradition. In the Septuagint (LXX), Adam is said to have begotten Seth at 230 years old, extending the pre-Flood total by several hundred years. The Samaritan Pentateuch has yet another set of numbers. Modern critical editions (BHS, BHQ) follow the Masoretic Text.

Calculation and Meaning of Absolute Dates

Thus, the birth of Seth is the first chronological anchor fixed within the Bible, but the calculation of absolute dates varies depending on which textual tradition is adopted.
